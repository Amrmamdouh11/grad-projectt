age: Represents the age of the individual.
gendera: Likely refers to gender (male or female).
BMI: Stands for Body Mass Index, a measure of body fat based on height and weight.
hypertensive: Indicates whether the individual has hypertension (high blood pressure).
atrialfibrillation: Indicates whether the individual has atrial fibrillation, an irregular heart rhythm.
CHD with no MI: Suggests coronary heart disease without a myocardial infarction (heart attack).
diabetes: Indicates whether the individual has diabetes.
deficiencyanemias: May refer to the presence of anemia due to nutritional deficiencies.
depression: Indicates whether the individual has symptoms of depression.
Hyperlipemia: Refers to high levels of lipids (fats) in the blood.
Renal failure: Indicates kidney failure.
COPD: Stands for Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease, a lung condition.
heart rate: Represents the heart rate in beats per minute.
Systolic blood pressure: The higher value in blood pressure measurement, representing pressure during heartbeats.
Diastolic blood pressure: The lower value in blood pressure measurement, representing pressure between heartbeats.
Respiratory rate: Indicates the number of breaths per minute.
temperature: Represents body temperature, possibly in degrees Celsius or Fahrenheit.
SP O2: Stands for Oxygen Saturation, indicating the percentage of oxygen in the blood.
Urine output: Indicates the amount of urine produced, possibly in a specific time period.
hematocrit: Measures the proportion of blood that is cellular (red blood cells).
RBC: Stands for Red Blood Cell count.
MCH: Stands for Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin, a measure of the average amount of hemoglobin in a red blood cell.
MCHC: Stands for Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin Concentration, a measure of the concentration of hemoglobin in a red blood cell.
MCV: Stands for Mean Corpuscular Volume, a measure of the average volume of a red blood cell.
RDW: Stands for Red Cell Distribution Width, a measure of the variation in red blood cell size.
Leucocyte: Represents the white blood cell count.
Platelets: Indicates the number of platelets in the blood.
Neutrophils, Basophils, Lymphocyte: Different types of white blood cells.
PT: Stands for Prothrombin Time, a measure of blood clotting.
INR: International Normalized Ratio, another measure of blood clotting.
NT-proBNP: N-terminal pro-B-type natriuretic peptide, a marker for heart failure.
Creatine kinase: An enzyme indicating muscle damage.
Creatinine: Indicates kidney function.
Urea nitrogen: Another indicator of kidney function.
glucose: Blood sugar level.
Blood potassium, Blood sodium, Blood calcium, Chloride: Electrolyte levels in the blood.
Anion gap: Measures the balance of ions in the blood.
Magnesium ion: Indicates magnesium levels.
PH: Represents the acidity or alkalinity of the blood.
Bicarbonate: Indicates the level of bicarbonate in the blood.
Lactic acid: Measures the level of lactic acid in the blood.
PCO2: Partial pressure of carbon dioxide in the blood.
EF: Ejection Fraction, a measure of heart function.
